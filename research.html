Wendy Williams

Research Interests

My research interests cover a wide range of Extragalactic Astronomy and Cosmology, including the formation and evolution of structure in the Universe and galaxy clusters. On smaller scales my interests include the nature and evolution of galaxies, their interactions, as well as their structure and composition with particular interest in the role of dust and gas in galaxies. As such, my interests cover a range of Infrared astronomy and Radio astronomy. I am also interested in HI studies and am excited about the HI and other surveys to be conducted with the SKA pathfinder telescopes, including the South African MeerKAT project.
I enjoy both observational and computational work.

PhD

I am doing my PhD under of Prof. Huub Rottgering at Leiden Observatory in the Netherlands. The goal of my project is to investigate the accretion modes of Radio Loud Active Galactic Nuclei (RL AGN), mainly using data from LOFAR. This is done by matching the radio sources to their multiwavelength counterparts and investigating their properties as a function of redshift.

Masters

My MSc research, under the supervision of Prof. R. C. Kraan-Korteweg was entitled “A multiwavelength study of HI galaxies discovered in the Zone of Avoidance”. The project entailed a near-infrared follow-up survey of the Parkes deep Zone of Avoidance galaxies for the purpose of using the Tully-Fisher relation to determine redshift-independent distances. The sample combineed HI spectral line data with new Near Infrared imaging. My work had a large observational component, including both Near Infrared observations using the Japanese Infrared Survey Facility (IRSF), a 1.4 m telescope in Sutherland, South Africa and 21 cm radio observations at Parkes, Australia.
This MSc work was funded by a scholarship from the South African SKA project.
Presentation given at the 2010 Astronomy Department Annual Student Seminar.

Honours

I completed my BSc (Hons) as part of the National Astrophysics and Space Science programme (NASSP). This involved a research component which I did under the supervision of A/Prof. P.A. Woudt (UCT) entitled "Galaxies in the Zone of Avoidance: An Infrared Study of HI Detections". The coursework included General Astrophysics, Electrodynamics, Computational Methods, Observational Techniques and Astrophysical Fluid Dynamics.
